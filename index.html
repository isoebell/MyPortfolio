{1} This is a zine, on ‘the forgotten art of a morning stretch,’ is an work of artistic satire. It is created to be observed and visually experienced, however it does not serve as a vessel for information learning. However, that is how it may present, a ‘how to guide’ of sorts. ChatGPT was used to produce the information on best practice and benefits of the morning stretch practice; a satire on artificial intelligence giving us ‘advice’ on how we should be moving our body, presented from the perspective of its own lived experience.

{2} A branding re-fresh of the social not-for-profit organisation ‘For Change Co.’ For Change Co. is a Victorian-based organisation that shares the vision of “an Australia free from youth homelessness.”

This refresh sought to reflect the organisation’s strong values, improve the efficacy of their design’s communication and become more visually engaging.

The simpler and more contemporary design approach separates the branding from a chain, creating a more personal brand identity. For this branding it is desirable that it feels like another Melbourne cafe, to be a more comfortable introductory working environment for the youth suffering from insecure housing. The goal is to not shine a light on this marginalised youth group; chain-like branding has the potential to highlight this, whereas this typographic-led approach to design creates a sense that it is another cafe in Melbourne.

{6} A book in a book in a book. This project consists of a book which documents a poster containing the first issue of the journal of the Situationist International (published June 1958). The typographic approach of the poster was inspired by All the World's a Page, with a collaged image of all the leftover packets of birth-control pills, and from painkillers post a surgery, and my parking fines. A personal and political commentary of sorts of all those institutions that continually take your money. This poster I pasted up in Brunswick, and continued to document it over time, this documentation, and a copy of the poster, are bound in this book. The cover of which is the typesetting of the journal without the collaged media overlayed.
